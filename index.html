Biography

Xuyang Cao is a Ph.D. candidate at Beijing Jiaotong University, supervised by professor Houjin Chen and associate professor Yanfen Li. He also works closely with professor Yahui Peng. His research area includes Image Processing and Machine Learning.

Recently, his research interests include machine learning, computer vision, medical image analysis, and artificial intelligence.

Projects

2019-Present Study on Breast Mass Detection, Classification and Parameter Measurement Methods using ABUS Images,  The National Natural Science Fund,   Participant
  • This project focuses on designing a computer aided diagnosis system for automated breast ultrasound (ABUS) images, including detection, segmentation, classification, and 3D reconstruction of breast tumors.
  • I mainly study the deep learning based breast tumor segmentation, including supervised segmentation, semi-supervised segmentation, neural architecture search, solving the class imbalance and large tumor size variations challenges, etc.
2018-Present Research on Detection and Classification of Lung Masses Based on MRI Images,   Participant
  • This project is to study the method of lung mass segmentation, multi-modality lung mass feature extraction, multi-modality feature fusion, benign and malignant classification of lung mass.
  • I mainly study the 2D MRI image segmentation of the mass in this project.
2017-2018 High Speed Train Gear Defect Detection Based on Computer Vision,   Horizontal Scientific Research Project,   Participant
  • This project provided a solution for detection and quantitative analysis of surface pitting in high-speed train gears, such as getting defect status of gear tooth surface, reporting statistic results, etc.
  • I was responsible for developing novel detection and segmentation algorithms as well as the software system.
2016-2017 Geometric Parameters Measurement of an Overhead Line System,   Participant
  • This Project provided a method and a device for measuring the geometric parameters of an overhead line system by using scale factors and frame differences.
  • I was responsible for the previous algorithm simulation and participated in the hardware structure design work.
